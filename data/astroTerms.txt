(1) forget all the weird index words below in column 2 -- instead, have arrays of words.  for example, instead of "adu", have "analog|digital|unit", 
so that every time an "adu" or "adus" is encountered, all three words in the index (analog, digital, unit) will record the location/paper/page/sector
of "adu".  

(2) when a search is conducted for a phrase, like analog_digital_unit, here's how to handle the extended search:  the extended search words should be
the UNION of the extended search words of those 3 words!!  Eliminates the need to put conditionals on the extended search like I was thinking of 
earlier, like adu=analog_digital_unit.  Just forget about doing that now.  The union takes care of EVREYTHING! 

(3) The reason we don't record arrays of words as index words for the spectral lines is because we need to have additional info regarding the 
lines available so that we can match up to other lines whose identities are not known OR lines that are actually the same as an indexed line but
maybe slightly different wavelength, so the code will recognize that indeed the lines match to within some delta and group them together.  HOWEVER, 
we can possibly use the "union" idea to get around the need for made-up spectral line identification.  In other words, instead of "HIL1.88" (how
the h-alpha line would be identifified), how about instead we record the page, sector, paper locations of an occurace of an h-alpha line into each 
of the following words: hydrogen, I, 1.88, allowed.  And then have a field called something like "supp" that would be an encoded array of all the
additional info we'd want to be able to retrieve about this line, say something like:  "0.05" which would be the accuracy of the energy value, and
this supp info would go into these 3 words (NOT all the other search words that might be involved with this line, like "optical", "spectral", "line", "balmer")
Although "hydrogen" will have a lot of various "supp" values, as will "I", and perhaps 1.88 will have a few (for exmaple, the nearby nitrogen
line could fall close enough for the '1.88 eV' bucket to catch it), the UNION of the "supp" field between these 3 words will produce exactly
1 match, the acccuracy of the halpha energy to be used to determine a match to a search for that line. 
For lines whose identities are not yet nown, they could be listed in the index in something like a HIL1343 syntax, which in and of itself would
indicate that the line had "unfinished business" and needs to be identified, so that a constant comparison to already-IDd lines in the index
would be done. 

I like the "clean" approach of the above, not cluttering the index with all these made-up words.
